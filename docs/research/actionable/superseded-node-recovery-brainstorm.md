# Problem: Valuable superseded nodes get buried

**Date**: 2026-03-14
**Context**: Auto-resolution sample review, finding #3

## The problem

When a node is superseded, it stays in the graph but is effectively invisible — excluded from queries, PageRank, and confidence computation. This is correct for nodes that are genuinely wrong or outdated. But some superseded nodes contain unique reasoning worth exploring — they lost because they had less structural support, not because they were wrong.

Example: fact-456 ("Collapse requires patterned entropy — a structured, resonant rupture — rather than mere random disturbance") was superseded by fact-069 ("The injection of randomness causes collapse, which enables the emergence of entropy and the flow of time") at a 116:0 ratio. The winner is well-supported. But the loser introduces "patterned entropy" — a concept not captured elsewhere in the graph — and may represent a refinement worth investigating further.

## The question

How do we surface superseded nodes that contain unique, potentially valuable ideas rather than just being weaker versions of the winner?
